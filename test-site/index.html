1984 toyota mr2 mk1: the most 80s thing that ever existed in the 80s.

James Bond doesn't drive a toyota. But, if you drop 60 pounds, don a black leather jacket with white japanese lettering, black leather pants, sport sunglasses, and hop into a mk1 Toyota mr2, you won't any longer mind british sensibilities. The light chassis sporting a lovely short wheel base and such touchy handling (snap oversteer) should evoke anything but images of a great british car. For them, there's nothing quite like a 4 meter long chassis, with a bleeding umbrella in the door pocket.

All of that is beside the point. The toyota mr2 is one of japans first real attempts at a sport car. It comes (broadly speaking) in three iterations

  • 1983-1989 mk1 mr2 (later years come with a factory turbo charger and rwd)
  • 1990-1997 mk2 mr2
  • 1997-2004 mk3 mr2
    •

    And stylistically, you can hardly tell they're related (save the mid-engine design).
